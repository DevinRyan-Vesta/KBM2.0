Why audit

An audit reconciles the key box on the wall with what KBM thinks is there. Run one monthly, after staff changes, or any time counts feel off.

Running an audit (admins)

  1. Go to Audits and click Create Audit. KBM snapshots every key in inventory, ordered by hook number.
  2. Optionally Print the audit sheet and walk the key box with a pen — or enter counts directly on the Input Results screen.
  3. For each hook, record how many copies are physically present.
  4. Complete the audit when every row is entered.
  5. Review the differences, then Apply to update the system counts to match reality. Applying is logged, so there's a record of every correction.
Audits are snapshots — keys added after the audit started won't appear in it. Finish and apply an audit reasonably promptly, or discrepancies pile up.

Low Copy Report

Lists every key whose total copies are at or below the threshold set in Settings — your "go cut more keys" shopping list. Exportable as CSV.

Reorganize

The Reorganize tool helps re-number key hooks (e.g. after physically rearranging the key box) by assigning new hook positions in bulk, grouped by property.
